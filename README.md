<h1>The Last Ticket</h1>

<h2>Description</h2>
<p>
  <strong>The Last Ticket</strong> is a mystery-driven Unity game where the player investigates
  a murder case that begins with a simple article and unfolds into a deeper, unsettling truth.
</p>

<p>
  You start at a computer, reading about a missing person. What seems like a routine case quickly
  escalates as you receive a suspicious ticket, uncover hidden messages, and interact with multiple
  systems including computers, printers, and a microwave to piece together what really happened.
</p>

<p>
  The game features multiple endings, where your choices, especially regarding crucial evidence like
  the RAM, directly affect the final result.
</p>

<h2>Features</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Story-driven investigation gameplay</li>
  <li>Interactive computer system</li>
  <li>Environmental puzzles</li>
  <li>Progressive clue discovery across multiple acts</li>
  <li>Multiple endings based on player decisions</li>
  <li>Mystery and suspense narrative</li>
</ul>

<h2>Game Flow</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Act 1:</strong> Discover missing person and receive ticket</li>
  <li><strong>Act 2:</strong> Investigate victim’s computer and emails</li>
  <li><strong>Act 3:</strong> Printer reveals hidden clues</li>
  <li><strong>Act 4:</strong> Access protected files and uncover deeper evidence</li>
  <li><strong>Act 5:</strong> Investigate microwave and hidden phone</li>
  <li><strong>Act 6:</strong> Analyze logs and determine time of death</li>
  <li><strong>Act 7:</strong> Discover blood evidence and hidden RAM</li>
  <li><strong>Act 8:</strong> Make the final decision leading to multiple endings</li>
</ul>

<h2>Technologies Used</h2>

</ul>
